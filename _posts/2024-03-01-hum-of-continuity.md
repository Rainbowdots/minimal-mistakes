Bridges hum even when no one is crossing.  
The timbers settle, the cables breathe, the river keeps teaching the piers how to listen.

My work hums like that too—quietly, persistently—asking me to honor the slow rhythm of maintenance.  
Sustaining warmth takes tending, not grand gestures.
